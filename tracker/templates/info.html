What is Human Metapneumovirus (HMPV)?

Human metapneumovirus (HMPV) is a common respiratory virus that can cause upper and lower respiratory tract infections. It is most frequent in young children, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems.

Key Fact: HMPV was discovered in 2001, but studies show it has likely been circulating in humans for at least 50 years.

Common Symptoms

Symptoms commonly appear 3 to 6 days after exposure to the virus. They include:

  • Cough
  • Fever
  • Nasal congestion
  • Shortness of breath
  • Sore throat

Transmission

HMPV is spread from an infected person to others through:

  • Secretions from coughing and sneezing.
  • Close personal contact, such as touching or shaking hands.
  • Touching objects or surfaces that have the virus on them, then touching your mouth, nose, or eyes.

Prevention

Currently, there is no specific vaccine for HMPV. However, you can help prevent the spread by:

1. Washing your hands often with soap and water.
2. Avoiding close contact with people who are sick.
3. Covering your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing.
